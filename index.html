The Legendary Samurai: Warriors of Ancient Japan

Written by Kenji Takahashi on Thursday, March 12th

For nearly a thousand years, samurai warriors represented the military nobility of feudal Japan. These skilled swordsmen lived by a strict moral code and helped shape Japanese culture in ways that continue to fascinate people around the world today.

In this feature, we'll examine the history of these legendary warriors and explore the lasting impact they've had on Japanese arts, philosophy, and modern entertainment.

Did You Know? The word "samurai" comes from the verb "saburau," which means "to serve"!

Who were the Samurai?

The term samurai refers to the military elite nobility of pre-industrial Japan. These warrior nobles served daimyo (feudal lords) and formed the ruling military class from the 12th century until their abolition in the 1870s during the Meiji Restoration.

Samurai were skilled in many combat techniques, with swordsmanship being particularly valued. They typically carried two swords—the longer katana and shorter wakizashi—which became potent symbols of their status. Beyond warfare, many samurai were also educated in literature, philosophy, and the arts.

Why do Samurai continue to captivate us?

The enduring fascination with samurai culture stems from several compelling aspects of their history and traditions

Even in times of peace, samurai would practice meditation and martial arts daily, perfecting both their spiritual discipline and combat techniques.

Absolute loyalty was demanded of all samurai. Harakiri was a ritual suicide method. Samurai were forbidden from showing pain. The Battle of Sekigahara united Japan.

Essential Samurai Weapons

  • Bladed Weapons
    • Katana - the primary long sword
    • Wakizashi - the shorter companion sword
    • Tanto - a dagger for close combat
  • Ranged Weapons
    • Yumi - the traditional longbow
    • Tessen - iron war fan
